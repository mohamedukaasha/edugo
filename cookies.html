What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help us remember your preferences and provide a more personalized, efficient experience.

At Edugo, we use cookies sparingly to ensure the minimalist nature of our platform remains uninterrupted by slow loading times or redundant inputs.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These are necessary for the platform to function correctly. They enable core behaviors like course progress tracking and secure terminal access.

Analytical Cookies

These help us understand how learners interact with our paths. We use this data to prune curriculum noise and improve instructional logic.

Preference Cookies

These allow the platform to remember choices you make, such as language settings or terminal themes, to provide an optimized environment.

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. However, if you limit the ability of websites to set cookies, you may worsen your overall user experience at Edugo.

  • Check your browser's Help menu for specific cookie controls.
  • Edugo respect "Do Not Track" signals from your terminal.
  • Blocking essential cookies will prevent platform access.

Updates to This Policy

We periodically refine our Cookie Policy to reflect changes in our technology stack or legal requirements. High-achieving professionals should check this page occasionally for updates.
